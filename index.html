About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the VLSI Information Processing (VIP) group at Cornell ECE, advised by Prof. Christoph Studer.

Original from Guatemala 游섫릖, I received a bachelor’s degree in Electronics Engineering at Universidad del Valle de Guatemala in 2016. Before studying abroad, I worked as a design engineer in a building automation company, Aktiva. In the Fall of 2016, I joined the VIP group at Cornell, where I started working on digital VLSI design of data detectors and precoders for massive MU-MIMO wireless systems. Currently, I am working on digital VLSI design for signal processing, wireless communication, machine learning, and emerging computer architectures.
